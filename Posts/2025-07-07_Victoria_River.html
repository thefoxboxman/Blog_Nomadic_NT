Victoria River Region

We are starting to head west again today. Leving Katherine heading towards Western Australia. It is a bit over 500km from Katherine to Kununurra; fifty odd kms on the other side of the border. The two day drive will take us across the mighty Victoria River region.

The highway sets out from Katherine across flat grassland plain before the craigy red ranges appear on the horizon.

At first the mountains are flat topped with plain slopes...

Then the dramatric red rock surfaces...

A super dramatic landscape...

About two-thirds of the way to Timber Creek we cross the fabled Victoria River...

We arrive at Timber Creek early afternoon. Just in time to secure one of the last caravan sites for the night. The caravan park is not bookable, it's a first come, first served basis.

The next morning we are before dawn to get to Policemans Point Lookout on the banks of the Victoria River for a sunrise shoot...

We time it perfectly...

We had some reports of good birding spots at Timber Creek. One is at the water tanks (leaks) bit up the hillside above the town. The road is sealed but steep and very windy. On the last hairpin bend we lost traction and spent a 10 hairy minutes on the end the drop until we were able to reverse down enough to get a runup. We made it finally but very emotionaly scared. Thankfully there were some birds to compensate for our near death experience...

The Policemans Point Lookout also returned some new birds for us...

After our adventures in Timber Creek we are heading to Kununurra to a few days. The escarpment around Timber Creek is still very scenic.

We are now entering the Boab country.

A Boab forest...
